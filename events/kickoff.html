Based on feedback from our last Kickoff, we are hosting sessions ranging from seminars to quick builds depending on participant interest. QFD Workshop

OK, you just saw the kick-off video. Now what? How do you get though all of the information, organize all of the chaos, and get your team in a direction for success in those first critical days? Using a combination of worksheets, strategy planning and QFD (Quality Function Deployment aka House of Quality), you and your team can head down the direction of success with all on board.

Ron Weber is a former cutting tool designer and manufacturer who has moved several years ago into the classroom at South Lyon East High School. Coach of the Team 3641, the Flying Toasters, since its formation, he has implemented many of the ideals of "building in quality" to the team's approach and success.

Robot Quick Build

The Robot Quick Build session is a great opportunity for younger teams to come together with FIRST alumni and build a FIRST standard kit bot. The goal is to walk out with a working kit robot and basic control system, some starter code, and a better knowledge of what it takes to build a successful robot. This is especially helpful for teams who don't have a lot of experience with FIRST! The students will be split into three subgroups — chassis assembly, control systems, and programming — and will be guided by mentors and alumni throughout the process. It is a good idea to figure out which students will be in which subgroup ahead of time. Mentors and coaches are encouraged to join!

Due to limited space, preference will be given to rookie and 2nd-year teams.

The full schedule and a list of things to bring will be sent out shortly. Each team will be required to bring their kit and safety glasses, as well a couple power drills if possible. Please feel free to email Amanda Burcroff at burcroff@umich.edu if you have any questions.
